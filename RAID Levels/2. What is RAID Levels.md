
# 🧠 What is RAID?

**RAID (Redundant Array of Independent Disks)** is a method that combines **multiple hard drives** to improve **speed**, **data safety**, or **both**. It is often used in servers or systems that store a lot of data.

---

## 🔍 Why Use RAID?

* To make your system **faster**
* To keep your **data safe**, even if one hard drive stops working
* To combine storage from multiple drives into one

---

## 🧱 RAID Levels – Explained Simply

There are different types of RAID called **RAID levels**. Each level has its own advantages and disadvantages. Here's a simple explanation:

---

### ⚡ RAID 0 – *Speed Only (No Protection)*

* **How it works**: Splits data into parts and saves it across all drives (called **striping**).
* **Good Side**:

  * Very **fast** read/write speed
  * Full use of storage (e.g., 2 x 1TB = 2TB)
* **Bad Side**:

  * If **one drive fails**, all data is lost
* **Best For**: Gaming, video editing (non-critical work)

---

### 🛡️ RAID 1 – *Mirror Copy for Safety*

* **How it works**: Makes an **exact copy** of your data on two or more drives.
* **Good Side**:

  * **Safe**: If one drive fails, your data is still on the other
  * **Good read speed**
* **Bad Side**:

  * You get only **half the total storage** (2 x 1TB = 1TB)
* **Best For**: Storing important files, backups

---

### 🧩 RAID 4 – *Stripe + Parity on One Disk*

* **How it works**: Saves data in blocks and keeps **parity** (backup info) on one drive.
* **Good Side**:

  * Can **recover data** if one disk fails
* **Bad Side**:

  * Slower when writing, because all write operations go through the parity drive
* **Best For**: Low-cost storage with some data protection

---

### 🔁 RAID 5 – *Stripe + Parity Spread Out*

* **How it works**: Like RAID 4, but **parity is shared** across all drives.
* **Good Side**:

  * Can survive **1 drive failure**
  * Better performance than RAID 4
* **Bad Side**:

  * Writing is **a bit slower** due to parity math
* **Best For**: Servers needing both **speed and safety**

---

### 🛡️ RAID 6 – *Extra Safety (Double Parity)*

* **How it works**: Like RAID 5, but can handle **2 drive failures**.
* **Good Side**:

  * Very **safe**, even if 2 drives fail
* **Bad Side**:

  * **Slower write speed**
  * Needs **minimum 4 drives**
* **Best For**: Enterprises with **critical data**

---

## 🔧 Less Common RAID Types

| RAID Level | Description                                   | Usage       |
| ---------- | --------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| **RAID 2** | Uses error correction (Hamming Code)          | Rarely used |
| **RAID 3** | Like RAID 4, but uses **byte-level striping** | Rare        |

---

## ✅ Summary – Which RAID to Choose?

* Use **RAID 0** for **speed**, but **no protection**
* Use **RAID 1** for **data safety**, not for big storage
* Use **RAID 5 or 6** for **both performance and safety**
* Avoid RAID 2 and RAID 3 — they are outdated
