
## 🖥️ HDD vs SSD 

**HDD (Hard Disk Drive)** and **SSD (Solid State Drive)** are two types of storage used in computers. They both store data like files, apps, and the operating system — but they work differently.

---

### 💽 **HDD (Hard Disk Drive)**

* **How it works**:
  HDDs have moving parts. Inside, they use spinning metal disks (called platters) and a read/write head (like a music player).

* **Speed**:
  Slower. Around **130 MB/s**, and it takes **1–2 seconds** to open files.

* **Storage Size**:
  Large and cheap. Usually from **1 TB to 8 TB**.

* **Best for**:
  Regular use like saving **documents, photos, movies**, and backups.

---

### ⚡ **SSD (Solid State Drive)**

* **How it works**:
  SSDs have **no moving parts**. They store data using **flash memory** (like a USB pen drive).

* **Speed**:
  Much faster. Speeds between **500 MB/s to 2000 MB/s**, and files open in **0.1 seconds**.

* **Storage Size**:
  Usually smaller (like **256 GB to 1 TB**) and more expensive.

* **Best for**:
  Tasks that need speed like **gaming, video editing**, or **graphic design**.

---

### 🔄 **Comparison Table**

| Feature         | HDD                  | SSD                    |
| --------------- | -------------------- | ---------------------- |
| 🔧 Moving Parts | Yes (spinning disks) | No (flash memory)      |
| 🚀 Speed        | Slower (\~130 MB/s)  | Faster (500–2000 MB/s) |
| 💾 Storage      | Larger (1–8 TB)      | Smaller (256 GB–1 TB)  |
| 💸 Cost         | Cheaper              | More expensive         |
| 🎯 Best Use     | Storing large files  | Fast apps, games, OS   |

---

### ✅ **Which One Should You Use?**

* Use **HDD** if you want **more space at a lower price**, and don’t need high speed.
* Use **SSD** if you want your computer to **start faster, open apps quickly**, and you don’t need huge storage.
