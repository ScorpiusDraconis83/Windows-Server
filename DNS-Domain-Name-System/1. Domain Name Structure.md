
### Story: **"Nikhil and His Domain Name"**

#### Beginning

In a small village, there lived a boy named Nikhil. Nikhil loved computers and the internet. He had heard that every website on the internet has a special name called a domain name. Nikhil thought, “Why don’t I create a domain name for myself?”

#### Structure of a Domain Name

Nikhil asked his grandfather about domain names. His grandfather explained, “A domain name is mainly divided into three parts. Let’s understand it with an example.”

1. **Top-Level Domain (TLD)**: This is the last part of the domain name, like `.com`, `.org`, or `.in`. It shows what kind of website it is. For example, in “nikhil.com,” the `.com` is the Top-Level Domain.

2. **Second-Level Domain (SLD)**: This is the main part of the name, like “nikhil” in “nikhil.com.” It is the unique name of the website.

3. **Subdomain**: This is the part before the main name, like “[www.”](http://www.”) So, in “[www.nikhil.com,”](http://www.nikhil.com,”) the “www” is a subdomain.

#### Nikhil’s Domain Name

Nikhil thought his domain name would be **"nikhilthecoder.com"**. Here:

* **"nikhilthecoder"** is the Second-Level Domain.
* **".com"** is the Top-Level Domain.

He told his friends, “When I type this name, people will be able to see my programming projects.”

#### Importance of Domain Names

Grandfather said, “The importance of a domain name is that it helps people reach your website. If you have a good domain name, people will remember you easily.”

#### Conclusion

Nikhil decided to register his domain name. He learned that a domain name is not just an identity, but it also gives him a special place on the internet. Now, Nikhil was ready to build his website with his new domain name.

### The End

Through this story, Nikhil understood the structure of a domain name in simple language. He learned how a domain name can become his identity and strengthen his presence on the internet.
