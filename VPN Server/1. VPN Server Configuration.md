
# What is VPN?

**VPN** means **Virtual Private Network**. It is a tool that helps you connect to the internet safely and privately.

When you use a VPN, your internet connection is secured and hidden from others, like hackers or your internet provider. It works like a secret tunnel between your device and the internet.

# How Does VPN Work?

Imagine you want to send a letter, but you don’t want anyone else to read it. So, you put the letter in a locked box and send it through a trusted friend.

A VPN does something similar with your internet data:

* It locks (encrypts) your data so no one can read it.
* It sends your data through a secure tunnel (VPN server).
* It hides your real location and IP address by using the VPN server’s address instead.

---

# Types of VPN

1. **Remote Access VPN**

   * Lets people connect safely to their work or school network from anywhere.
   * Example: You work from home and need to access files from your office.

2. **Site-to-Site VPN**

   * Connects two different offices over the internet.
   * Example: A company with offices in New York and London can connect their networks securely.

3. **Client-Based VPN**

   * Requires special software on your computer or phone to connect.
   * Example: You install a VPN app on your phone to protect your internet connection.

---

# Benefits of Using VPN

* **Protects Your Privacy**: Your online actions stay secret.
* **Access Blocked Websites**: You can visit websites that might be blocked in your country.
* **Secure Online Shopping and Banking**: Keeps your payment info safe from hackers.

---

# How to Use a VPN (Example)

1. Download a VPN app like **ExpressVPN** or **NordVPN** on your phone or computer.
2. Create an account or log in.
3. Choose a country from the list of servers.
4. Click **Connect**.
5. Now, you can browse the internet safely and privately!

---

# Summary: How VPN Protects You

* When you visit a website, your request goes through the VPN server first.
* The website sees the VPN server’s address, not yours.
* Your data is locked (encrypted) so no one else can read it while it travels.
