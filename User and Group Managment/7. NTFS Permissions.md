
# NTFS (New Technology File System) 

NTFS is the main file system used by Windows to organize and protect your files and folders.

---

### What Are NTFS Permissions?

**NTFS permissions** decide who can do what with a file or folder. They control who can open, change, or delete files — helping keep your data safe.

---

### Types of NTFS Permissions (What You Can Allow People to Do)

| Permission       | What It Means                                                 | Example                                                            |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Full Control** | Can do everything: open, edit, delete, and change permissions | You can open, change, delete a file, and also let others access it |
| **Read**         | Can only open and view files, not change anything             | You can view a photo but can’t delete or edit it                   |
| **Write**        | Can add or change content but not read or delete              | You can add text to a document but not open it fully               |
| **Execute**      | Can run a program or script but not open or change it         | You can run a game or app but not edit its files                   |
| **Delete**       | Can remove the file or folder                                 | You can delete a file but not open or edit it                      |

---

### How to Set NTFS Permissions (Step-by-Step)

1. **Right-click the file or folder** you want to protect or share.

2. Select **Properties** from the menu.

3. Click the **Security** tab.

4. Press the **Edit** button to change permissions.

5. Choose the **user or group** you want to give permissions to (like "Users" or "Administrators").

6. Check the boxes next to the permissions you want to give (like Read, Write, Full Control).

7. Click **OK** to save.

---

### Simple Example

Imagine you have a folder named **"School Projects"** on your computer:

* You want your little sibling to **only read** the files, so they can see the projects but not change or delete them.
* You want your friend to **have Full Control** because they are helping you edit the projects.

Using NTFS permissions, you can set this easily:

* Give your sibling **Read** permission.
* Give your friend **Full Control** permission.

Now, everyone has exactly the access they need!

---

### Summary

NTFS permissions help you control who can:

* See your files,
* Change your files,
* Delete your files,
* Or even run programs.

This keeps your important data safe and organized.
